Lasagna

Lasagna has roots tracing back to ancient times, with the earliest known recipe found in a 14th-century English cookbook. The word "lasagna" originates from the Greek word "lasanon," meaning a cooking pot or vessel. Throughout history, lasagna evolved with regional variations emerging across Italy, featuring ingredients like béchamel sauce in Emilia-Romagna and ricotta cheese in Southern Italy. Italian immigrants spread lasagna's popularity worldwide in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, making it a beloved comfort food globally. Today, lasagna remains a timeless classic in Italian cuisine, cherished for its comforting layers of pasta, cheese, and sauce.

Ingredients to make Lasagna

  • Lasagna noodles (either pre-cooked or oven-ready)
  • Ground meat (such as beef, pork, or turkey)
  • Tomato sauce or marinara sauce
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese (shredded)
  • Parmesan cheese (grated)
  • Eggs (to bind the ricotta cheese)
  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Olive oil
  • Italian seasoning or herbs (such as basil, oregano, and parsley)
  • Salt and pepper
  • Optional: vegetables like spinach, mushrooms, or bell peppers
  • Optional: additional seasonings or spices to taste, like red pepper flakes or fennel seeds

Directions

  1. Prepare the ingredients by gathering lasagna noodles, meat, tomato sauce, cheeses, vegetables, and seasonings.
  2. Preheat the oven to the temperature specified in your recipe, typically around 375°F (190°C).
  3. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions until al dente, then drain and set aside.
  4. Prepare the sauce by browning ground meat (if using) in a large skillet, adding chopped onions, garlic, and other vegetables, pouring in tomato sauce and seasonings, then simmering for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Prepare the cheese mixture by combining ricotta cheese, eggs, grated Parmesan cheese, and any additional seasonings in a separate bowl.
  6. Assemble the lasagna by spreading a thin layer of the meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish, layering with cooked lasagna noodles, then spreading a layer of the cheese mixture on top, and repeating the layers until all ingredients are used, ending with a layer of sauce on top.
  7. Add cheese: Sprinkle shredded mozzarella cheese on top of the final layer of sauce.
  8. Bake: Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for about 30 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.
  9. Rest and serve: Let the lasagna rest for about 10 minutes before slicing and serving. This allows it to set and makes it easier to cut into neat portions.
  10. Enjoy your delicious homemade lasagna served with a side salad and garlic bread!
